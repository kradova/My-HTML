The interior of the theater is festive and elegant. The architecture of the auditorium, designed for 1664 seats, is designed in the late French "rococo" style. It is luxuriously decorated with various stucco ornaments with fine gilding. The ceiling plafond is decorated with four paintings by the artist Lefler in the form of medallions. They depict scenes from Shakespeare's works: Hamlet, A Midsummer Night's Dream, A Winter's Tale and As You Like It. In the center of the ceiling is a large crystal chandelier. There is a wide variety of stucco decorations in the hall (the stucco molding in tiers, side vestibules and along the stairs leading to the lodges is done with special grace), lamps, candelabra and ornaments of bronze furniture inlays. The central curtain was made according to the sketch of the famous theater artist Alexander Golovin. In the auditorium, spectators are located in the stalls, boxes of the benoir, mezzanine, 1st and 2nd tiers and in the gallery. Among the boxes, the "royal" box stands out - in the center of the mezzanine directly opposite the stage. It has 12 seats in the front row.
